Some of my work from the past year:

I calculated and visualized popular clothing trends according to categorized Instagram posts from a Cornell dataset using d3.js and Python to process the clothing attributes. I worked alongside a very capable Vihar Desu.

I visualized geoclustered opioid drug transactions in my home state of NJ with a very smart team of three (Ben Choi, Jessie Korovin). Our work was recognized as the top-ranked project in our "Cloud Computing with Big Data Applications" class. We used Spark (running on Amazon EMR), Python, and matplotlib to perform a k-means clustering on the data.

Over the summer, I worked with some awesome people at Eluvio to demonstrate the superior performance of their video streaming platform using React.

This year, I also participated in the CalHacks hackathon with Frank Wang on a rideOs challenge. We visualized ride-sharing pickup and dropoff data in Chicago to get an understanding of how to optimize ride-sharing fleet software.

In the past, I've also:

Created a real-estate tech startup MVP that scanned in client contracts, then populated a personalized calender with relevant events and sent out scheduled emails to the necessary contractors. We built it using Django, a free OCR API, Amazon s3 and Bootstrap.
